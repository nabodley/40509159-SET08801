Pigeon Island


Pigeon Island was inhabited by the Arawaks and Carib around 1000 A.D. In the middle of the 16th century a french pirate named Francois le Clerc, used the island as his base. two peaks the island is a historic site with numerous forts such as an 18th-century British fort and Fort Rodney both used by the British to spy on French Ships from neighbouring island Martinique. In the 1700s Admiral Rodney took over the Pigeon Island and made it his base.
